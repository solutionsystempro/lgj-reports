Pre-Call Prep

2 minutes before · Never skip
Mental Checklist
  • 1
    Review lead source

    DM thread, VSL buyer, referral? Warm leads have higher DFY potential — flag them.

  • 2
    Know their business in one sentence

    Agency? SaaS? Consultant? Revenue range? Fills the "Situation" section faster.

  • 3
    Set your tone anchor: Calm. Curious. Non-reactive.

    You are a doctor — not a closer. You diagnose. If it's a fit, you prescribe.

  • 4
    Set a 45-minute hard mental timer

    If you're at minute 35 and haven't transitioned to pitch — you've rabbit-holed. Reset.

Opening & Frame

Target: ≤ 2 minutes · No small talk loops
Rapport — 60 Seconds Max

One genuine question. One exchange. Move on.

"What's been a highlight of your week so far — anything good going on?"
[Listen, mirror briefly, pivot immediately to frame]
Frame the Call — Non-Negotiable
"Here's how I want to use our time together — I'm going to ask you some questions about where you're at and where you want to be. If what you share tells me we can actually help, I'll walk you through exactly what that looks like. If it's not a fit, I'll tell you straight. Does that work?"

⚡ Why this matters: This one line sets you as the authority and prevents prospects from hijacking the call. If they say "can you just send info?" — you've already framed why that wastes their time.

① North Star — Define the Destination
"What outcome are you actually going after in the next three to six months — like what does success look like if we fast-forward?"
"And when you say [their answer] — what does that mean for you personally? Why does that number matter?"

📍 Write their answer down verbatim. You will mirror it back during the pitch and close.

② Situation — Current State & Numbers
NEPQ Connecting
"Walk me through what you're currently doing to get clients right now."
"How many calls or leads are you generating per month on average?"
"And where does most of that come from — referrals, ads, outbound, something else?"
"It sounds like things are going fairly well on [their best channel]. Is there anything you'd change about that if you could?"

🎯 The Two Truths Question (Miner): Disarms defensiveness. They reveal the crack in their own "everything's fine" story.

③ Problem Awareness — Surface the Pain
Do NOT skip. Do NOT rush.
"Do you like those results?"
"What's specifically not working the way you want it to?"
"When did you first notice that was a problem?"
"How long have you been dealing with that?"
"And how does that affect you day-to-day — like what does a week look like when the pipeline's dry?"

🔑 Patience is power here. Don't jump to solutions. Silence after these questions forces them to fill the space with emotion. That emotion is what closes the sale.

④ Solution Awareness — Surface Doubt in Their DIY
NEPQ Solution Awareness
"What have you tried so far to fix that?"
"Why not keep doing it that way — what's stopping that from working long-term?"
"If you had a magic wand for your outbound right now — which part would you fix first: the tech, the copy, or the targeting?"
"And what's kept you from getting that handled already?"

💡 Negative question (Mason / Cole Gordon): "Why not keep doing it that way?" forces them to disqualify their own current approach — not you.

⑤ Consequence Questions — Make the Cost Real
NEPQ Consequence · Most skipped = most kills
"What's that costing you every month that this isn't solved — in actual dollars, best estimate?"
"What happens to the business if nothing changes in the next six months?"
"And twelve months from now — if you're still in the same spot — what does that look like for you?"
"How long can you realistically afford to stay where you are?"

🔥 Tony Robbins principle: Pain is a greater motivator than pleasure. These questions don't create pain — they surface the pain they're already living with. Let them answer fully. Do not rescue them.

⑥ Future Vision — Paint the Other Side
"Now flip it — what does life look like when this is actually solved? Twelve months from now, fully fixed."
"What does [their revenue goal] allow you to do that you can't do right now?"

🌟 Let them paint the picture. You'll use their exact words in the pitch. "You said you want [their words]..." — that's the close.

⑦ Commitment Check — Qualify Readiness
"Is solving this a nice-to-have for you right now, or is it genuinely a must?"
"On a scale of 1–10, how important is figuring this out in the next 30 days?"
"What would need to be true for you to be a clear yes on whatever we put in front of you today?"

⚠️ If they answer below 7 or say "nice to have" — do not pitch. Return to consequence questions. They haven't bought the problem yet.

Transition Language

Bridge from discovery to pitch · 30 seconds
Template
"That's really all I needed to hear. Based on everything you've shared with me, I do think the Insiders program can help you [their North Star goal]. If it would help, I can walk you through exactly how we'd solve [their top pain] — and you can decide if it makes sense. Sound good?"
DFY Upgrade Read (Hormozi offer framing)

After transition — if they said "I don't have time to implement" or "I want this done fast" during discovery, insert:

"Before I walk you through the Insiders program — based on what you've told me about [their situation], we also have a done-for-you option where Jay and the team build the entire system for you. I want to show you both so you can decide what makes more sense."

⚡ Feb Pattern Fix: This is the DFY upsell window you missed. Don't wait until after they say no to Insiders. Introduce the option at the transition.

"And you also get lifetime access — so there's no subscription, no expiry date, no retainer trap. One investment, keep it forever."

Tie-Down Sequence

Execute after pitch · Before price · Every time
  • 1
    Certainty Check

    "Based on what we've covered — do you feel this can actually get you to [their North Star goal]?"
    Wait for a yes. Silence if needed. Do not bail them out.

  • 2
    Verbalize the Why

    "What specifically makes you feel that way?"
    Gets them to sell themselves. Their answer = your close language.

  • 3
    Investment Statement

    "The investment to make this happen is $2,000 paid in full — or we have a two-pay option at $1,100 each."
    State it matter-of-factly. Do not apologize. Do not add fluff.

  • 4
    Decision Ask — Say it once, shut up

    "What would you like to do next?"
    The first person to speak after this question loses. It won't be you.

Tie-Down Rule

If they hesitate on step 1 certainty check — do not present price. Go back to discovery: "Help me understand what's making you unsure — is it the approach or something about your situation?"

Objection Handling

Cushion → Clarify → Isolate → Reframe → Close
The 5-Step Framework — Run It Every Time
StepPurposeLanguage
1. CushionLower defenses"Totally hear you." / "Fair question." / "Makes sense you'd feel that way."
2. ClarifyFind the real concern"When you say [X], what do you mean exactly?"
3. IsolateConfirm it's the only blocker"Aside from [X], is there anything else holding you back?"
4. ReframeShift perspective or identitySee specific scripts below
5. CloseMove to next step"Does that address it, or is there something else?"
"That's too expensive."
Diagnose
"I hear you — let me ask, if money wasn't the issue at all, would you be confident this is the right fit?"
If YES → it's not a money issue, it's a belief issue. Address the real concern.
If NO → they're not sold on the solution. Return to discovery.
Reframe
"So it sounds like it's not really about the $2,000 — what else is missing for you to feel fully confident?"
Cost of Inaction
"You mentioned this is costing you around [their number] every month. If nothing changes in the next six months, that's [math]. The $2,000 pays for itself on the first new client. Does that math land for you?"

Identity Reframes

Jeremy Miner NEPQ · Use for Level 3–4 objections

Use these when the objection is really about how they see themselves — not the product.

TriggerReframe
"I'm not sure I can follow through." "The fact that you're still on this call and being honest about that tells me something different. People who can't follow through don't worry about follow-through."
"I'm just not the type who does these programs." "What type of person do you want to be? Because the results you're after require a different version of you than the one who's been stuck on referrals for [X months]."
"Maybe in a few months." "What's happening in a few months that isn't happening right now? And what changes in your situation between now and then?"
"What if I fail?" "What does failing look like specifically? Because you already have a 14-day guarantee — worst case you get a refund and keep everything you learn in week one."
