The Origin — How It Started

Community Optimizer → Setter → Closer → Revenue Architect
Ian Kirk joined Lead Gen Jay in late 2024 as a community optimizer — his initial scope was funnel architecture and Skool community engagement, not sales. The original agreement: $2,500 upfront + $500/month retainer, with $100 per Insiders close and $250 per high-ticket close as bonuses. He was contractor #3. The first two hires were already in place.
The first closer on the team — contractor #2 — was handling all sales. Ian's initial job was to optimize the community funnel feeding that closer. He learned the product, the community, and the prospects from the inside. Then something happened: demand outpaced capacity. The existing closer got booked out two weeks. People who wanted to buy couldn't get on a call fast enough.
Jay asked Ian to start getting on calls. Ian said yes. That moment changed the entire trajectory of the team.

Driving revenue architecture from the closer seat

Course Pricing Architecture
Ian 2.5x'd the Average Transaction Value
Before Ian joined, the Insiders Course sold at $389 full pay with $499 split pay options — and averaged $542 per transaction across 1,167 deals. Ian advocated for and helped architect a complete pricing overhaul. Full pay rose to $1,497. Ian introduced the concept of charging a premium for split pays — moving from $499 to $549 split — shifting the incentive structure so full pay was rewarded. The result: during Ian's tenure the average transaction hit $1,268 across 496 deals, nearly the same total revenue from roughly half the transaction volume.
