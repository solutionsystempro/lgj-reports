When Andrew Nagle departed, he offered Ian an opportunity to sell for him. Ian declined — following Mason's direct coaching not to split focus or hop opportunities. Stayed committed to LGJ and the process being built here.

When asked to step into a bigger sales role, Ian was the one who asked to bring Mason on to help develop into it properly. Invested in the team's infrastructure — not just his own numbers.
